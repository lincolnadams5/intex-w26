# Executive Summary: Donor Inactivity Risk Model

## What This Model Does

This model scores every active donor on a scale of 0–1, representing their probability of going inactive within the next 90 days. A score of 0.85 means the model estimates an 85% chance that donor will stop giving within 90 days.

Board members and admins review a weekly prioritized list (sorted highest score first) and conduct personal outreach calls to high-risk donors before the relationship lapses.

---

## Business Problem

Retaining an existing donor costs far less than acquiring a new one. The challenge is identifying *which* donors are quietly drifting toward inactivity before it happens — so the team can act proactively rather than reactively.

---

## How the Model Works

The model learns patterns from donor history across two sources:

| Feature category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Donor profile | Supporter type, acquisition channel, relationship type, region |
| Donation behavior | Total donations, average donation value, whether recurring, campaign count, donation type mix |
| Tenure | How long they've been a supporter, average gap between donations |

It does **not** simply flag donors who have already missed 90 days — that would be reactive. Instead, it uses the above behavioral profile to identify donors who *look like* donors who historically went inactive, before the 90-day mark is reached.

---

## Key Findings

- **Most predictive factors:** Recurring status, acquisition channel, and supporter type are the strongest predictors of inactivity risk
- **Highest-risk segments:** One-time campaign donors and social media-acquired supporters show higher inactivity rates than church-referred or recurring donors
- **Model type:** Logistic Regression — chosen for explainability and low resource footprint

## How to Use the Risk List

1. Run inference weekly: `python -m jobs.run_inference`
2. Query `donor_risk_scores` in Supabase, sorted by `risk_score` descending
3. Focus outreach calls on donors with `risk_score ≥ 0.60` (adjust threshold based on team capacity)
4. A false positive (calling a loyal donor unnecessarily) is a low-cost mistake — a personal call rarely harms a relationship

---

Retrain quarterly, or when the donor count grows by 50%. Run `python -m jobs.train_model` to refresh.
