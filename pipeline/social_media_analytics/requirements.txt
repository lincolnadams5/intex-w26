My read on the scope:

A true causal analysis (difference-in-differences, causal forests) requires experimental or quasi-experimental data. What you actually have is observational data, which is perfect for explanatory regression + descriptive analytics — that's the honest framing and what a professor will want to see. The deliverable is: "here's what correlates with high donation value and why, based on historical posts."

The recommendation dashboard is appropriate scope and is honestly the most impressive deliverable of the three pipelines. Instead of a post optimizer based on prediction (which requires a predictive model), it becomes a "what worked" dashboard — staff pick a post type/platform/tone and see what similar historical posts generated, plus regression-derived coefficients telling them what characteristics drive donation value.
